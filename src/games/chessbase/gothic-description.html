Ed Trice invented the game of Gothic Chess in the year 2000 after researching 80-square board configurations for two years.

His analysis was very thorough, deemed worthy enough to be published in ICGA Journal in June of 2004.

(See "80-Square Chess," International Computer Games Association Journal Vol. 27 No. 2 pp. 81-95, 2004)

Trice demonstrated that there are flaws present in Capablanca's Chess (circa 1925) which tried to remedy

issues with Henry Bird's Chess (circa 1874), and Bird may have been trying to fix aberrations with

Pietro Carrera's 80-square configuration dating back to 1617.

Trice determined that the Gothic Chess configuration, the only one with all of the pawns defended

from the starting position, is the one with the greatest symmetry, and is the most enjoyable setup to play.
